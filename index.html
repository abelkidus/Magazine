On December 12, 2016, on the UEFA's UCL round of 16 draw, RUUD GULLIT made the draw that set the stage for a match between Unai Emery's PSG and Luis Enrique's Barcelona, to be played on February 14th and March 8th. After 2 months of unbearable wait, it was matchday. Forty Thousand Barcelona fans travelled to Paris to watch their team play against the hosts Paris Saint-German. The stadium Parc-des-Princes filled up 3 hours before the game even started. The whole stadium was very excited. The match started, both teams were playing an even game until the 15th minute. PSG got a freekick outside the Barca's penality box. Ángel Di María was chosen to take the freekick. The birthday boy didn't let his team down, he scored a beautiful freekick. 25 minutes later, at the 40th minute, Julian Draxler scored another goal which gave the home team a 2 - nil lead before half time. The frustrated Barcelona fans waited 15 minutes for the second half hoping their team would bounce back. After the second half started, Barcelona's attacking trio MSN(Messi, Neymar and Suarez) tried repeatedly, but they weren't lucky enough to score against the PSG goalkeeper. At the 55th minute, Ángel Di María scored his 2nd goal of the match and PSG were leading 3-0. Barcelona were in the muds. Just as they thought it couldn't get any worse, Edinson Cavani scored the final goal of the match and gave his team a 4-0 victory over the Spanish gaints. PSG players and fans celebrated their huge victory all night.

3 weeks later, it was time for the 2nd leg. This time Barcelona were playing in their home, Camp Nou. Hours before the game, in an interview Barcelona's manager Luis Enrique was asked how he was planning to comeback from a 4-0 defeat, he simply replied "If they can score 4, we can score 6". This mad him sound delusional, but he was hopeful and he had a plan. The game was about to start and there were about a hundred thousand fans, Ninty thousand of them were Barcelona fans. The 90K fans waving the red and blue flag started cheering when their team entered the stadium, the noise so loud that it would have been unlikely to found a PSG player who wasn't nerveous. The match started and it only took 3 minutes for Luis Suarez to find the net, Barcelona scored an early goal and they were hungry for more. Barcelona were playing a beautiful game, but they couldn't convert their shots. In the 40th minute, Paris SaintGermain's Layvin Kurzawa scored an own goal in an attempt to block a shot by Andrés Iniesta. Barcelona went to half time leading 2-0 in the match and only needing 2 goals to make it 4-4 on aggregate. The second half started and PSG were yet to make an attempt on Barca's goal. In the 50th minute Neymar Jr. was fouled inside PSG's box, and the referee gave a penality to Barcelona, and Lionel Messi scored it with power. The game became 4-3 on aggregate. Barca looked like they were actually going to make it, they were one goal away from forcing the game to extra-time. Then Edinson Cavani with the first glimpse of goal he had, shocked the whole stadium by scoring a goal in the 62nd minute. Because of the away goal rule, Barca now needed an extra three goals if they are going to beat PSG. It seemed like there was no coming back from this, but the Blugranasstill didn't lose their hope. They just quickly restarted the game and marched to PSG's penality box, but couldn't score a goal. Time was running out, remaining time went from 30 minutes to 20 then 10, 5 and suddenly it was the 88th minute, Neymar gets fouled outside PSG's box, Neymar takes the freekick and scores a mesmerizing goal from athight angle. Barcelona were 2 goals down to achieve the impossible. The assistant referee showed additional time of 5 minutes, time was ticking. Barcelona were at it again, Messi crosses the ball and Suarez gets fouled inside PSG's box for a penality. Neymar steps up again and scores Barca's fifth goal in the 92nd minute. For the next 3 minutes, the whole studium were up on their feet. Barca fans were praying for another miracle to seal the deal. It's Neymar again, he gets a freekick, this time it was a little bit to the left of the center-circle, 20 seconds were left to the match, Neymar crossed the ball but Adrian Rabiot cleared it with a header. Neymar gets the ball again, he chipped the ball over the PSG defense, Sergio Roberto out of nowhere reaches with his left foot, and BOOM! The whole stadium, the whole world, went wild. Barcelona did the impossible with only seconds left on the watch. Luis Enrique run to the pitch to celebrate with his team. The players joined the fans with celebration. Everybody in the stadium witnessed an unforgattable night and Neymar,the man of the match, imprintedhis name into the hearts of Barca fans.

La Remontada, it's Spanish for "comeback". It was a word nobody knew about, but now whenever a football fan hears that word, they instantly think ofthis extraordinary night.

He was the greatest of Dutch artists. Rembrandt renowned for his versatility and technical mastery, but most of all for the emotional depth and subtlety of his work. The son of prosperous Miller, Rembrandt was born in leiden, then the second larg town in Dutch republic. He showed brilliant talent from an early age. After he settled in the capital, Amsterdam, in 1631 or 1632, he quickly became the leading portraitist in the city, his career was successful for another decade, but in the 1640s his worldly fortunes declined as he began to work more to please himself than his wealthy clients. At this time, he had personal problems, too, not least the death of his beloved wife saskia in 1642. in the 1650s he had increasing financial troubles and narrowly escaped bankruptcy,but he recovered with the help of his mistress, Hendrickje,and his son, Titus .The premature death marred his final years, but the quality of his work remained undimmed. In addition to the portrait that were his mainstays,Rembrandt excelled in religious subjects and made occasional but memorable contributions in other fields, including landscapeand still life. He was also a superb and prolific draftsman and by common consent the greatest of all etchers. Self portrait

He battered by life's blows, but still proud and defiant, Rembrandt gazes out of an image of noble authority. He painted himself many times, but this is perhaps the most imposing of all himself portraits. Earlier in his career, he had often portrayed himself flamboyantly, playing out some role in fancy costumes or showing off his success in expensive clothes. Now, however, nearing 60, he is content to Express the dignity of his profession. The picture feeling of monumental strength stems partly from the breadth of his brush work. Indeed, the handling is so sketchy virtually everywhere except the head that many experts think the painting is unfinished

He was an artist of great diversity, so it is likely that he had not one but several reasons for portraying himself. In his youth he provided a convenient and free model for trying out facial expressions, for example, and later he was probably inclined at times to celebrate his success and social status. It is also likely that his self portraits found ready market for even in his financially strained later years he remained an admired figure. very title is recorded about the whereabouts of his self portraits in his own lifetime

# Friends: The Show That Defined a Generation
Friends is one of the most successful and beloved sitcoms of all time. It aired for 10 seasons from 1994 to 2004, and followed the lives of six friends in their 20s and 30s living in New York City. The show was created by David Crane and Marta Kauffman, and starred Jennifer Aniston, Courteney Cox, Lisa Kudrow, Matt LeBlanc, Matthew Perry, and David Schwimmer. The show was known for its witty humor, relatable characters, iconic catchphrases, and memorable moments. It also tackled various issues such as career, romance, family, marriage, divorce, and parenthood. The show won six Emmy Awards, including Outstanding Comedy Series in 2002, and ranked among the top ten of the final television season ratings. Friends had a huge impact on popular culture and influenced many aspects of society. It inspired fashion trends, hairstyles, music, books, movies, and even language. Theshow also spawned a spin-off series called Joey, which starred Matt LeBlanc as his character Joey Tribbiani. In 2021, the cast reunited for a special episode titled Friends: The Reunion, which featured interviews, behind-the-scenes footage, and celebrity guests. Friends is more than just a sitcom; it is a phenomenon thathas touched millions of fans around the world. It is a show that celebrates friendship, love, laughter, and life. It is a show that has made us laugh, cry, and feel. It is a show that we will always be there for.
